---
title: "Privacy & Public Profile"
---

Your Ranked tracker is personal. Everything you log or import stays private to
your account, and nobody else can see it unless you decide to share a summary.

Ranked is private by default on purpose. Unlike scrim data, which you record so
your team and coaches can review it together, your ranked history is a record of
your own climb — and your win rate, slumps, and hero pool are nobody's business
but yours unless you choose otherwise. So nothing is published until you
explicitly turn it on, rather than the other way around.

<Callout type="info">
  Ranked stats are private by default. You have to opt in before any of your
  ranked data is shown publicly.
</Callout>

## What is private

By default, all of your ranked data is for your eyes only:

- The full dashboard at `/ranked`, including every tab and chart.
- Your individual game log and the match form.
- Season, patch, and role breakdowns.

None of this is exposed on your public profile or anywhere else until you turn
sharing on.

## Publishing a summary

If you want to show off your competitive progress, you can opt in to a public
ranked summary. The toggle lives in your account settings on the
[Linked Accounts](/docs/settings/linked-accounts) page, in the Ranked section.

When you enable it, a summary of your ranked stats becomes visible on your
[public profile](/docs/profile/overview). Flip the toggle off at any time to make
your ranked data private again. The change takes effect right away.

<Callout type="info">
  Enabling the public summary publishes an overview of your ranked
  performance — not your full game-by-game history. The detailed dashboard at
  `/ranked` always stays private to you.
</Callout>

The public summary is intentionally a curated highlight — think overall win
rate, your top heroes, and recent form — rather than the full thirty-chart
dashboard. The idea is to let you show your progress at a glance without
exposing every individual game, every map breakdown, and every off-meta
experiment to anyone who visits your profile.

## Changing your mind

Privacy here is fully reversible. Turning the toggle on and off does not delete
or alter any of your data — it only controls whether the summary appears on your
profile. You can switch it as often as you like.
