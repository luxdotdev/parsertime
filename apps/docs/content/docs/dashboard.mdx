title: "Dashboard"
---

The dashboard is the first thing you see when you sign in. It's your home
base for getting into your work: a recent-activity overview at the top,
your list of [scrims](/docs/scrims) below it, and the
[team switcher](/docs/teams) tying it all together. Pick a team to focus on
one roster, or leave the switcher on all teams to see everything at once.

## Recent Activity Overview

Above the scrim list sits a collapsible overview band that summarizes how
things are going at a glance, so you don't have to dig into individual
scrims to get the headline numbers. What it shows depends on whether a
team is selected.

### With a Team Selected

When you're focused on a single team, the overview shows:

- **Win rate**: the team's overall win rate, with its win–loss record and
  an up/down indicator when the rate has shifted week over week.
- **Maps logged**: how many maps have been recorded for this team.
- **Team TSR**: the team's [Tournament Skill Rating](/docs/tsr/team-tsr), along with
  how many players on the roster are rated and the confidence in the
  figure.
- **Best map**: your strongest map by win rate, with that win rate shown
  beneath it.

Below the cards, a **Win rate · weekly** area chart plots the team's win
rate over recent weeks against a 50% reference line, so you can see
whether the team is trending up or down.

### With No Team Selected

When the switcher is on all teams, the overview switches to a cross-team
summary:

- **Scrims logged**: total scrims across all your teams.
- **Maps logged**: total maps across all your teams.
- **Active teams**: how many teams have at least one scrim.
- **Latest scrim**: when your most recent scrim was logged.

Below the cards, a **Scrims logged · weekly** area chart shows your scrim
volume over recent weeks, a quick read on how active you've been.

## Collapsing the Overview

If you'd rather keep the focus on your scrim list, collapse the overview
with the chevron on the right edge of the band. Collapsed, it shrinks to
a single line of compact figures with the charts and detail hidden.
Expand it again the same way.

Your choice is remembered between visits, so the dashboard opens the way
you left it.

<Callout type="info">
    If the overview can't load its data, it simply hides itself and
    leaves your scrim list as the main surface. Nothing else on the
    dashboard is affected.
</Callout>
